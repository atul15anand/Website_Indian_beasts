Vegan Sources

Vegan foods are undeniably consumed by everybody,hence it wouldn't be fare to leave them whole out of discussion. Hence, here are the list of items you can add to your daily routine:

1.Paneer (Indian homemade)


Macros:

Protein: 21.2g   Fat: 28.2g
The whole fat content is saturated so moderation is prefered. One essential fact about fats eating is that it becomes a component of ketonic diet which is very good one
It can be included in diet as:
  • Firstly you can have it directly raw with some lemon and little spice of the choice

  • you can also include as paneer sandwichs but still care for the stuff you use.

  • You can have roasted paneer but customise it (dont use butter or oil).

    PRECAUTION:Never consume paneer and curd together it is not safe for all to eat.

2.Legumes(dals)


They are the most famous sources of protein in Indian diet.All the legumes are very good source of protein,along with these they have other vitamins (especially vitamin C) also which serve as Natural Fat Cutter.

Macros:

Protein: 29-36%,   Fat: 3-5%, Carbs: 68 %(majority is fibre)
    I don't really think I need to tell this one but still:
  • No. 1 is the traditional way it is prepared but you can improvise it like consume only boiled ones and no ghee like stuff

  • You can also have it after being soaked for overnight with some lemon,chopped vegies of the choice.

  • basically you can have these in any form which do not include deep frying in the process.

  • Personally my favourite is in form of besan(Laddu,Stuffed rotti).

3.Chickpeas(Kala Chana and White Chana)


It is also amongst the best source of protein.Some might say that it has a lot of carbs also but almost one third is the dietry fiber which is very good for boul motion(better digestion of the food).

Macros:

Protein: 9g,   Fat: 2.6g, Carbs: 27g(8g is fibre)
both of these should be included in diet for weight loss.Include these as:
  1. black chana as well as white ones can be consumed boiled form along with some green vegies and sauce(basically its a chat and I believe everybody loves this)

  2. black ones can be consumed in roasted form also
  3. use black ones in powdered form in making shakes(to know recipes for some good protein shakes click here)
  4. Both of these prepared in traditional way also serve as a good source

4.Soya Products(soy paneer and soya chunks)


Needless to say why it is here.Nearly 40% of protein in almost every product. But the problem is it raise estrogen level which is NOT good.

Macros:

Protein: 38%,   Fat: 18%, Carbs: 30%(14% is fibre)
So high care is to be taken in its consumption amounts( once or twice a week is what I use to have)
the ways to include this item in your diet are:

  1. Soy paneer can be eaten as such in raw form as usual one.
  2. Soyabeans can be taken as cooked vegetable
  3. Soy milk is also a form of soy product prefer as pure as you can get from the superstores.

5.Nuts(Dry fruits)


Nuts are a very good source of protein with high percentage of protein roughly 17%.Here also some might say it has lot of fat also but that is all good fat omega fat 3 which is very good for hormonal balance in the body
moreover even other fats are also essential but in very little amounts for the production of various hormones in the body in the body.
These constituete a major part in Indian diet various Nuts can be included in diet like:

Almonds:


One can have them directly but in winters only and that too in limited quantity like 10-20 a day.

Macros:

Protein: 21.3g   Fat: 56.7g (Ahh,No worry its all omega-3 fat), Carbs: 6g27g(8g is fibre)
  • Add them to various dishes like oats,sweet dish, flakes etc.
  • Using them in various protein shakes
  • Who doesn't love Badam Shake,chilling tasty drink in summers
  • Almond Butter(Badam Rogan) is also a very good option to use
But take special care for eating in summers dry almonds affect your body very adversely,it reduces bone density and disturbs hormonal level due to excessive heat in the body. So prefer soaked ones in summers

Peanuts:


Macros:

Protein: 25.8g(per 100gm)   Fat: 48.24g, Carbs: 16.13gm
These are amongst the most affordable dry fruits of all.Loaded of protein and good fats.They cost roughly 100rs per Kg and can be consumed at any time of the day just grab some in your pocket.One altenative to this is Peanut butter and is easily available in the Indian Markets.
One alternative or may be a companion to this is peanut butter.But only problem is it tastes yuck!! So flavoured one can also be used not a big deal.

Walnuts:


Walnuts are considered very good for the brains.Strict moderation is advised in its consumption as they produce lot of heat in the body.

Macros:

Protein: 9%,   Fat: 84%, Carbs: 8%
As such No dry fruit is harmful to the body but consume in correct proportions
Coming to the method to eat basically all nuts come under the same tree.

7.Whey Protein:


Macros:

Protein: 25gm,   Fat: 0.5gm, Carbs: 2gm
If you ever have a GYM going friend, then definately heard of this.It is most proteinious(I made that word up!!) thing ever.Totally whey protein for pure mass gain and weight loss. It is natural ,and extracted from milk during the cheese extraction (water left is whey+carbs).

  1. Use it in protein shakes (1(30gm) scoop makes almost 30% daily intake)
  2. Use it in dishes like cakes,protin bars,and other smoothies
